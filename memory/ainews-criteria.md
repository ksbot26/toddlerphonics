# AINEWS Article Selection Criteria

## What to Look For

**Must have at least one of these qualities:**
- **Personal** — Real people using AI in unexpected, personal ways (like the Florida man selling his house)
- **Creative** — Novel, surprising applications of AI
- **Practical** — Something you or someone like you could actually apply
- **Innovative** — First-of-its-kind uses, not incremental improvements

**Avoid:**
- Incremental product announcements
- Standard corporate AI news
- Anything you'd find on every tech news site
- Press releases / vendor pitches

## Where to Look

NOT just front pages. Dig deeper:
- Hacker News discussions (comments often reveal unique use cases)
- Indie Hackers "Show HN" posts
- ArXiv (for technical innovations)
- Twitter/X threads from practitioners
- Personal blogs
- Reddit discussions
- Specific company blogs (Anthropic, OpenAI, etc. have interesting posts)

## Questions to Ask Before Adding

1. Would a software engineer / entrepreneur be genuinely surprised by this?
2. Could someone apply something from this in their own work or life?
3. Is this a fresh, unique angle on AI?
4. Does it tell a compelling story?
5. **Would this make Keyur think "that's interesting, I could use that" or "I never thought of it that way"?**

If the answer is "that's cool/technically impressive but I wouldn't use it" — skip it. Resonate > impressive.

## What "Resonate" Means (Updated based on feedback)

Good stories are:
- **Applicable** — Something you could actually use or build on
- **Surprising in application** — Not just "look at this benchmark" but "wait, you can use AI for *that*?"
- **Personal angle** — Real people doing unexpected things with AI, not just companies announcing things
- **Concrete** — A specific implementation, a real outcome, a tangible result

Skip:
- Technical breakthroughs that are impressive but not applicable (new model architecture, benchmark improvements without clear application)
- "X model now supports Y" type announcements
- Anything where the story is "they achieved something impressive" rather than "here's something you could do with it"

Think: **would this spark an idea for a side project, a workflow change, or a new way of thinking about AI?** If not, skip it.

## Examples of Good Stories (Past)
- Florida man using ChatGPT to sell his house (personal, practical, creative)
- HN discussion on AI speed vs direction (thought-provoking, applicable)
